1. Welcome & Mission

Welcome to Myriad Evo. We’re a team focused on producing more sales, engagement, and visibility for every client we serve. We do this through three pillars: Innovation (new ways to grow), Scalability (solutions that expand), and Impact (measurable results).

This handbook is written for workers and recruits. It explains what we expect, how you’ll be evaluated, and how you earn — while pointing to the full partner/investor handbook for strategic context.

2. Onboarding & First 6 Months

Target group: students and early-career talents who learn fast and align with our mission.

What to expect in your first week
  • Welcome call with your recruiter and lead.
  • Account setup: Notion, Trello, Airtable, GitHub access.
  • Receive 2–3 small tasks (learn-by-doing).
  • Assigned a mentor for questions and feedback.
First 6 months
  • Task-based pay only (details in Payments section).
  • Frequent feedback cycles — weekly check-ins for month 1, then biweekly.
  • Encouraged to submit ideas from day 1.
  • End-of-6-months review determines eligibility for permanent contract.

3. Work Models & How We Operate

There are two main ways we deliver value:

  1. Existing Clients (Continuous Improvement) — we already delivered a project and now improve it over time. Partners and workers propose features, implement, and get rewarded.
  2. New Clients (Fresh Projects) — we analyze needs, create a roadmap broken into features, and publish features internally for implementation.

4. Ideas & Feature Workflow

We value ideas. To keep things fair and fast, follow this workflow:

Step A — Submission

Submit a short pitch (1–3 lines) describing the idea and expected impact. If possible, upload a small prototype, screenshots, or a mock.

Step B — Review

The review team evaluates viability, alignment with sales/engagement/visibility, and technical feasibility. Ideas are categorized (Basic/Gold/Premium) and given a priority.

Step C — Implementation

Ideas become public in the internal task board. Any team member can request to implement. Multiple implementations are allowed; the best one wins. Contributions are tracked in the partner ledger.

Step D — Client Pitch

The agency chooses whether to pitch to the client. If the client approves, the feature is sold and payments are distributed per the Payments section.

Step E — Recognition

If your idea is not chosen, you still receive documented credit. If your implementation is not selected, you may receive recognition credits depending on the review.

5. Payments & Revenue Sharing

Clear rules keep rewards fair. Payments are logged and visible to affected contributors.

Default split (when feature is purchased by client):
  • Idea author(s): 30%
  • Executor(s): 70%
  • If the same person(s) did both: 100%

Basic features: paid from maintenance fees or retainer pools.

Gold & Premium features: paid when the client purchases the feature. Percentages above apply.

Payment cadence & method

  • Payments are made monthly in the local currency agreed in your onboarding.
  • All payouts are logged and visible in the operations dashboard.
  • Taxes and local legal deductions are the responsibility of the worker unless otherwise specified in a contract.

6. Growth Path & Reviews

We want people to grow with us. Growth steps:

  • Continuous feedback & mentoring.
  • 6-month formal review covering: task contributions, code quality, ideas, reliability, and client impact.
  • Top performers offered permanent roles with base salary + revenue sharing.
What we measure in reviews
  • Delivery: how consistently do you finish tasks on time?
  • Quality: code & design standards, testing, maintainability.
  • Ideas: number and quality of ideas submitted and their impact.
  • Collaboration: communication, mentoring, and teamwork.

7. Culture & Code of Collaboration

Culture is how we work every day. Key rules:

  • Respect: communicate professionally and respectfully.
  • Transparency: all submissions, reviews, and payments are logged.
  • No sabotage: no theft of work, no sabotage, no personal attacks.
  • Credit & ownership: ideas become agency property on submission, but authors always keep credit and share.
  • Help others: mentoring and code reviews are encouraged.
Consequences

Repeated violations of conduct policies may result in removal from the partner program or termination of contracts. Formal steps include warning, remediation, and, if needed, removal.

8. Operations & Tools

Early-stage tools (what you'll use today): Notion, Airtable, Trello, GitHub Issues. These track ideas, features, payments, and partner stats.

How we track work

  1. Ideas are filed in Ideas Board with short pitch + attachments.
  2. Roadmaps and features live in the Project Roadmap.
  3. Implementations are open tasks in GitHub Issues or task-board.
  4. Payments and partner history are logged in Airtable/ledger.

Long term: we will develop an internal platform with AI-assisted review and automation to speed categorization, dispute resolution, and payments.

9. Conflict Resolution & Fairness

We aim for transparent, documented decisions.

9.1 Categorization disputes

If you feel your idea was undervalued, you can appeal. Appeals are reviewed by the lead examiner and optionally by AI-assisted analysis. The final decision is binding and documented with reasons.

9.2 Execution disputes

Competing implementations are judged on quality, scalability, and alignment. The chosen solution is published with a summary of the decision. Non-chosen contributors may receive recognition credits.

9.3 Idea ownership

On submission, ideas become agency property. Authors retain credit and share rights (30%) if implemented, even if someone else executes the work.

9.4 Transparency

All decisions, reviews and payments are recorded. Your partner history is accessible to you in the operations dashboard.

10. FAQs

Q: How do I get paid for small bug fixes?

A: Small bug fixes are generally part of maintenance and paid from maintenance pools. If a fix is sold as a feature, it follows the idea/execution split.

Q: Can I work on my own projects while partnered?

A: Usually yes, but inform your lead if there is potential competition with Myriad Evo clients or IP. We expect transparency.

Q: Who owns the IP I write?

A: Work done for Myriad Evo is owned by the agency once delivered under contract. Exceptions and specifics will be written in your contract.

Q: How do I appeal a payment decision?

A: File an appeal using the internal appeals form. The lead examiner will review, and you will receive documented reasoning.
